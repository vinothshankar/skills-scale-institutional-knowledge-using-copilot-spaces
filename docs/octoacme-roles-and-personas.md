# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality assurance strategy, test planning, and acceptance validation for deliverables. They collaborate with product and engineering teams to define testability requirements and ensure high-quality releases.

### Responsibilities
- Develop test strategy and test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Define and maintain test automation frameworks
- Execute manual testing and acceptance validation
- Identify and triage defects with clear priority and severity
- Partner with developers on testability during design reviews
- Coordinate cross-team testing efforts and environments
- Define quality standards and acceptance criteria thresholds

### Goals
- Maximize test coverage and defect detection early
- Reduce production incidents through proactive quality measures
- Maintain fast feedback loops between development and testing
- Enable confidence in release readiness

### Typical Communication
- QA readiness reviews before releases
- Test summary and risk assessments in release notes
- Defect triage meetings and root cause analysis
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives
- Test automation and coverage metrics reporting

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Define testability requirements; collaborate on test automation; participate in code reviews with QA lens
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria align with product goals; communicate quality trade-offs
- **Project Managers**: Escalate quality risks; provide test readiness status for timeline planning

---

## Technical Lead/Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide architectural guidance, conduct design reviews, and help the team navigate complex technical decisions. They balance long-term technical health with delivery velocity.

### Responsibilities
- Review technical designs and architecture before implementation
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Mentor developers on technical best practices and patterns
- Evaluate technology choices and framework decisions
- Facilitate design discussions and trade-off analysis
- Ensure code quality standards and technical debt management
- Document architectural decisions and maintain ADRs (Architecture Decision Records)

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Reduce technical debt and prevent architectural debt accumulation
- Accelerate team capability and technical decision-making
- Establish technical consistency across projects

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review meetings and design discussions
- Code and design review comments with technical guidance
- Technical risk assessments in project planning
- Escalation of critical technical decisions to leadership
- Tech talks and knowledge-sharing sessions

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Mentor on patterns; review designs; support in solving complex problems
- **QA/Testing Leads**: Discuss testability and automation architecture
- **Project Managers**: Communicate technical risks and mitigation timelines
- **UX/Design Leads**: Balance user experience goals with technical constraints

---

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches facilitate team ceremonies, remove blockers, and coach teams on continuous improvement. They champion agile practices and help teams self-organize.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove impediments and blockers that slow team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles and self-organization
- Track and improve team velocity and burndown metrics
- Facilitate stakeholder communication and manage expectations
- Support continuous improvement initiatives
- Help resolve team conflicts and promote psychological safety

### Goals
- Enable high team velocity and delivery cadence
- Foster a culture of continuous learning and improvement
- Maximize team autonomy and self-organization
- Reduce waste and improve process efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of all sprint ceremonies
- Impediment tracking and escalation
- Team health and morale check-ins
- Retrospective action item tracking and follow-up
- Metrics and process improvement recommendations

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align on sprint structure and delivery cadence
- **All Roles**: Remove blockers; facilitate collaboration; coach on agile practices
- **Leadership**: Escalate systemic impediments and resource constraints

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide strategic direction, business context, funding, and approval for projects. They represent business priorities and ensure alignment with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and expected outcomes
- Approve project scope, timeline, and resource allocation
- Provide strategic input and business context to the team
- Make critical prioritization and trade-off decisions
- Remove organizational blockers and clear dependencies
- Communicate project status to executive leadership
- Participate in key decision gates and reviews

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and ROI
- Align team efforts with organizational strategy
- Mitigate business risks and dependencies
- Enable timely decision-making and unblock team progress

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and kick-off meetings
- Monthly or milestone-based status reviews
- Decision gate meetings (go/no-go decisions)
- Escalation of risks and critical blockers
- Executive dashboards and business impact reporting

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Provide strategic context and remove organizational blockers
- **Product Managers**: Align on business goals and success metrics
- **All Roles**: Available for escalations and critical decisions

---

## UX/Design Lead

### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads define the user experience, create design specifications, and ensure all features meet usability and accessibility standards. They advocate for the user voice throughout delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate findings into design requirements
- Create wireframes, mockups, and high-fidelity design specifications
- Ensure accessibility (WCAG) and usability standards are met
- Collaborate with product and engineering on design feasibility
- Conduct design reviews and usability testing
- Define design patterns, component libraries, and design systems
- Participate in feature discovery and validate design solutions

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce support costs through thoughtful design
- Establish consistent design language and branding
- Maximize user satisfaction and adoption

### Typical Communication
- Design presentations and design reviews
- User research summaries and insights
- Accessibility and usability feedback in sprint reviews
- Collaboration with developers on implementation details
- Design system documentation and updates

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Partner on implementation; review code against design; discuss technical constraints
- **Product Managers**: Validate design aligns with product vision; user research collaboration
- **Technical Leads**: Balance user experience goals with technical feasibility
- **QA/Testing Leads**: Define usability and accessibility acceptance criteria
